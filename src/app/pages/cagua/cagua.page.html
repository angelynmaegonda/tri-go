Cagua Volcano

Cagua Volcano, Cagayan

Cagua Volcano is a 1133 meter-high stratovolcano located in the northernmost part of the Sierra Madre mountain range.

It is one of the active volcanoes in the country and has erupted twice in recorded history. Its last eruption was in 1907.

The forested volcano is locally known as the "Mountain of Fire".

It has six hot springs: Maasok near the crater, Marafil, Manaring, San Jose, Kabinlangan, and Paminta.
