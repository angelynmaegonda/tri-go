ARTICLES

LUZON

REGION I: ILOCOS REGION
Occupying the northwestern section of Luzon and part of Central Luzon plain, the region comprises four provinces: Ilocos Norte, Ilocos Sur, La Union, and Pangasinan.
CORDILLERA ADMINISTRATIVE REGION (CAR)
Also known as the Cordillera Region, or simply, Cordillera, it is the only landlocked region in the insular country. It is the least populous region in the Philippines, with a population less than that of the city of Manila.
REGION II: CAGAYAN VALLEY
Located in the northeastern section of Luzon Island, it is composed of five Philippine provinces: Batanes, Cagayan, Isabela, Nueva Vizcaya, and Quirino. Most of the land area is situated on the valley between the Cordilleras and the Sierra Madre mountain ranges. Cagayan Valley is the second largest Philippine administrative region by land area.
REGION III: CENTRAL LUZON
Primarily serving to organize the 7 provinces of the vast central plains of the island of Luzon, the region contains the largest plain in the country and produces most of the country's rice supply, earning itself the nickname, "Rice Granary of the Philippines". Its provinces are: Aurora, Bataan, Bulacan, Nueva Ecija, Pampanga, Tarlac, and Zambales.
NATIONAL CAPITAL REGION (NCR)
Also known as Metro Manila, it is the seat of government and one of three defined metropolitan areas in the Philippines (the others being Metro Cebu and Metro Davao). It is composed of 16 cities: the cities of Manila, Quezon City, Caloocan, Las Piñas, Makati, Malabon, Mandaluyong, Marikina, Muntinlupa, Navotas, Parañaque, Pasay, Pasig, San Juan, Taguig, and Valenzuela, as well as the municipality of Pateros. With a population of 13.4 million people, it is the second most populous, and the most densely populated region in the Philippines. The region is the center of culture, economy, education and government of the Philippines.
REGION IV-A: CALABARZON
Formally known as the Southern Tagalog Mainland, the region comprises five provinces: Cavite, Laguna, Batangas, Rizal, and Quezon; The region is the most populous region in the Philippines, having over 14.4 million inhabitants by 2020, and is also the country's second most densely populated after the National Capital Region.
REGION IV-B: MIMAROPA
Also known as the Southwestern Tagalog Region, it is one of two regions in the country having no land border with another region (the other being Eastern Visayas). The name is an acronym, a combination of its constituent provinces: Mindoro (divided into Occidental Mindoro and Oriental Mindoro), Marinduque, Romblon, and Palawan.
REGION V: BICOL
Bicol comprises six provinces, four on the Bicol Peninsula mainland (the southeastern end of Luzon) - Albay, Camarines Norte, Camarines Sur, and Sorsogon - and the offshore island provinces of Catanduanes and Masbate. The Philippines' major religion, Roman Catholicism, has the highest number of followers in the region than any other area in the country.

VISAYAS

REGION VI: WESTERN VISAYAS
It consists of six provinces: Aklan, Antique, Capiz, Guimaras, Iloilo, and Negros Occidental. The region is dominated by the native speakers of four Visayan languages: Hiligaynon, Kinaray-a, Aklanon, and Capiznon. With a population of 7.9 million inhabitants, it is the second most populous region in Visayas, after Central Visayas.
REGION VII: CENTRAL VISAYAS
It consists of four provinces: Cebu, Bohol, Negros Oriental, and Siquijor. Major islands are Cebu, Bohol, and Siquijor, together with the eastern part of Negros. The region is dominated by the native speakers of four Visayan languages: Cebuano, Bantayanon, Boholano, and Porohanon. With a population of over 8 million inhabitants, it is the most populous region in Visayas.
REGION VIII: EASTERN VISAYAS
It consists of three main islands: Samar, Leyte, and Biliran, and comprises six provinces: Biliran, Leyte, Northern Samar, Samar, Eastern Samar, and Southern Leyte. The region's most famous landmark is the San Juanico Bridge, which links the provinces of Samar and Leyte. With a population of 4.5 million inhabitants, it is the third most populous region in Visayas.

MINDANAO

REGION IX: ZAMBOANGA PENINSULA
It consists of three provinces: Zamboanga del Norte, Zamboanga Sibugay, and Zamboanga del Sur. The region was previously known as Western Mindanao. It is also known for its vintas, a traditional and colorful outrigger boat from the Philippine island of Mindanao.
REGION X: NORTHERN MINDANAO
It comprises five provinces: Bukidnon, Camiguin, Misamis Occidental, Misamis Oriental, and Lanao del Norte, all occupying the north-central part of Mindanao island, and the island-province of Camiguin. The region is dominated by the Cebuano ethnic group, with the Cebuano language being its lingua franca.
REGION XI: DAVAO REGION
It is situated at the southeastern portion of Mindanao and comprises five provinces: Davao de Oro, Davao del Norte, Davao del Sur, Davao Oriental and Davao Occidental. With a population of 5.2 million people, it is the most populous region in Mindanao.
REGION XII: SOCCKSKSARGEN
Located in south-central Mindanao, its name is an acronym that stands for the region's four provinces - South Cotabato, Cotabato, Sultan Kudarat, Sarangani, - and one highly urbanized city - General Santos. The region has extensive coastlines, valleys and mountain ranges. Known for its river system, the region is the drainage basin of Mindanao, particularly at the Cotabato Basin.
REGION XIII: CARAGA ADMINISTRATIVE REGION
The region occupies the northeastern section of Mindanao and comprises five provinces: Agusan del Norte, Agusan del Sur, Dinagat Islands, Surigao del Norte, and Surigao del Sur. It features several beaches, abundant seafood, hot and cold springs, evergreen forests, and balmy weather. Caraga's most famous attraction today is Siargao Island.
BANGSAMORO AUTONOMOUS REGION IN MUSLIM MINDANAO (BARMM)
Located in the Mindanao island group of the Philippines, it consists of five predominantly Muslim provinces: Basilan, Lanao del Sur, Maguindanao, Sulu, and Tawi-Tawi. It is the only region that has its own government and the only Muslim-majority autonomous region in the Philippines.
